Daniel Funes founded The Funes Law Group, P.A. on a simple belief: immigration law is ultimately about people — their families, their businesses, and their futures. Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Answers to what clients most often ask before their first consultation.

How much does an immigration consultation cost?
Consultation fees vary by case type. During your call, our team will confirm pricing and explain exactly what to expect before you commit to anything.
Do you handle cases outside of Florida?
Immigration law is federal, so we can represent clients nationwide and internationally, with our office based in Miami, Florida.
Is your team bilingual?
Yes. Attorney Daniel Funes and our staff communicate fluently in both English and Spanish throughout every stage of your case.
How long does a marriage-based green card take?
Timelines vary based on whether the petitioner is a U.S. citizen or permanent resident, and current USCIS processing times. We'll walk through a realistic timeline specific to your case.
Can you help if I'm already in removal proceedings?
Yes. We represent clients in bond hearings and removal defense, including waiver and humanitarian relief applications where eligible.
What's the difference between the E-1 and E-2 visa?
The E-1 is for treaty traders conducting substantial trade with the U.S., while the E-2 is for treaty investors making a qualifying business investment. We'll help determine which fits your goals.
